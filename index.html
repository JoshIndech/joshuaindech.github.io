Discover Georgia: A Road Trip Through Nature and History

Georgia is full of hidden gems that make for unforgettable road trips. Whether you're craving the tranquility of the mountains or the rich culture of the coast, our state offers experiences that are both scenic and accessible. Let us take you on a journey that blends natural beauty with Southern charm.

From the North Georgia Mountains to Savannah's Historic Streets

Start your adventure in Amicalola Falls State Park, located in Dawsonville. This stunning park is home to Georgia’s tallest cascading waterfall and is a gateway to the Appalachian Trail. The park offers a range of trails for all skill levels, and the Amicalola Falls Lodge is perfect for travelers who want a cozy overnight stay. For campers, the park provides well-maintained campsites and backcountry shelters. Hiking to the base of the 729-foot waterfall is a must for first-time visitors.

Next, head southeast toward Savannah, one of the most picturesque cities in the South. As you stroll through its cobblestone streets and shady squares, you'll be transported back in time. Don’t miss a tour of the historic district or a riverboat ride along the Savannah River. For a truly local experience, stop by Forsyth Park and explore the nearby shops and eateries.

Along the way, you can rest and refuel at Georgia Welcome Centers, which offer clean rest areas, maps, and expert travel advice.

Whether you're looking for outdoor exploration or cultural immersion, Georgia offers the perfect blend. Pack your bags and hit the road. You’ll be glad you did.
